Frequently Asked Questions

How does Duplicate Photo Finder work? +

Duplicate Photo Finder uses advanced on-device AI and machine learning to scan your photo library and identify duplicate and similar photos. The app employs three detection methods:

  • Exact Matching: Identifies identical photos (same file)
  • Perceptual Matching: Finds photos that look the same even if resized or slightly modified
  • AI-Powered Visual Similarity: Detects visually similar images like burst shots and near-identical photos

All processing happens entirely on your device. Your photos never leave your iPhone.

Is my privacy protected? Do you upload my photos? +

Your privacy is completely protected. We do not collect, upload, transmit, or sell your photos. All scanning, comparison, and duplicate detection happens entirely on your device using local AI processing.

We only collect minimal technical data (like crash reports and usage analytics) to improve the app. Your photos never leave your iPhone under any circumstances.

How fast is the scanning process? +

Duplicate Photo Finder is extremely fast. Most users can scan 1,000+ photos in less than 30 seconds, depending on:

  • Total number of photos in your library
  • Device model and available RAM
  • Photo resolution and file sizes
  • iOS version and system load

The on-device AI processing makes it much faster than cloud-based solutions.

How does the app select which photos to keep? +

Duplicate Photo Finder automatically ranks and selects the best photo in each duplicate group based on:

  • Resolution: Higher resolution photos are preferred
  • Sharpness: Sharper images are ranked higher
  • File Format: Prefers JPEG over HEIF for compatibility
  • Overall Quality: AI analysis of image quality

You maintain full control. You can review all recommendations and manually select which photos to delete before confirming.

What happens if I accidentally delete important photos? +

Don't worry! Deleted photos don't disappear immediately. Here's how recovery works:

  • Deleted items go to your Photos app's "Recently Deleted" album
  • You have 30 days to recover them before permanent deletion
  • Recovery is one-tap: open Photos app → Recently Deleted → Recover
  • The app also provides one-tap undo functionality

Recommendation: Always back up important photos to iCloud or other backup services before deleting.

Does the app support video duplicates? +

Yes! Duplicate Photo Finder detects duplicate videos alongside photos. The app:

  • Scans your entire photo and video library
  • Identifies duplicate video files
  • Ranks videos by resolution, codec, and file size
  • Provides safe deletion with 30-day recovery

Both Pro Monthly and Pro Lifetime plans include full video support.

How do I manage my subscription? +

All subscriptions are managed through your Apple ID. To manage or cancel:

  1. Open Settings on your iPhone
  2. Tap [Your Name] at the top
  3. Select Subscriptions
  4. Find Duplicate Photo Finder
  5. Tap Manage to change your plan
  6. Tap Cancel Subscription to stop renewing

Changes take effect immediately or at the end of your billing period, depending on your action.

The app won't access my photos. What should I do? +

If the app can't access your photo library, follow these steps:

  1. Open Settings on your iPhone
  2. Scroll down and find Duplicate Photo Finder
  3. Tap Photos or Library
  4. Select "All Photos" or "Full Access"
  5. Return to the app and try scanning again

Note: The app requires full photo library access to detect duplicates across your entire library.

What iOS versions does the app support? +

Duplicate Photo Finder requires:

  • iOS 16.0 or later on iPhones
  • iPadOS 16.0 or later on iPads
  • macOS 13.0 or later on Mac (M1 chip or later)

We recommend keeping your device updated to iOS 17 or later for best performance and features.

How much storage does the app use? +

Duplicate Photo Finder is very lightweight:

  • App Size: Only 10.2 MB
  • Installation: Minimal device storage required
  • Cache: Minimal temporary data (automatically cleared)

The app doesn't store your photos—it processes them on-device and only uses storage for its own code and temporary processing.

Will using the app drain my battery? +

Scanning will use some battery depending on your library size, but it's generally efficient:

  • 1,000 photos typically uses 5-10% battery
  • Larger libraries (10,000+) may use more
  • On-device processing is more battery-efficient than cloud alternatives
  • Tip: Plug in your device for optimal scanning

The app doesn't run in the background or continuously monitor your photos.

Troubleshooting

App Crashes or Won't Open

  1. Force close the app by swiping up from the bottom
  2. Wait a few seconds and reopen it
  3. If issues persist, try restarting your iPhone
  4. Update to the latest iOS version in Settings → General → Software Update
  5. Delete and reinstall the app from the App Store

Slow Scanning or Freezing

  1. Close other apps running in the background
  2. Ensure you have at least 2GB of available storage
  3. Restart your iPhone
  4. Try scanning a smaller album first (Photos app → Create Album)
  5. Update to the latest app version from the App Store

Missing Photos from Scan Results

  1. Ensure the app has Full Access to your photo library
  2. Check Settings → Privacy & Security → Photos → Duplicate Photo Finder
  3. Photos in iCloud Photos-only may take longer to scan
  4. Recently deleted photos won't appear in duplicate detection
